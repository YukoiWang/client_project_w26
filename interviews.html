Interview Preparation

Prepare to Shine in Your Interviews

Interviews can feel intimidating, but with proper preparation, they become opportunities to showcase your skills, experiences, and fit for the role. Whether you're preparing for your first internship interview or a full-time position, these resources will help you approach interviews with confidence and authenticity.

UMSI students bring unique skills and perspectives to the table. This page will help you articulate your value, answer common interview questions, and demonstrate how your UMSI education prepares you for success in the workplace.

Interview Preparation Steps

  1. Research the Organization: Learn about the company's mission, values, recent news, and culture. Understand the role and how it fits into the organization.
  2. Review the Job Description: Identify key skills and qualifications required, and prepare examples that demonstrate your fit.
  3. Prepare Your Stories: Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to prepare examples that showcase your skills and experiences.
  4. Practice Common Questions: Rehearse answers to typical interview questions, but keep your responses natural and authentic.
  5. Prepare Your Own Questions: Develop thoughtful questions to ask the interviewer about the role, team, and organization.
  6. Test Your Technology: For virtual interviews, test your camera, microphone, and internet connection in advance.
  7. Plan Your Logistics: Confirm the interview time, location (or virtual link), and plan to arrive or log in 5-10 minutes early.

Types of Interviews

  • Behavioral Interviews: Focus on past experiences and how you've handled situations. Use the STAR method to structure your responses.
  • Technical Interviews: Assess your technical skills and problem-solving abilities. Practice coding challenges, case studies, or technical scenarios relevant to your field.
  • Case Interviews: Present you with a business problem to solve. Practice breaking down problems, asking clarifying questions, and communicating your thought process.
  • Panel Interviews: Involve multiple interviewers. Make eye contact with all panel members and address questions to the person who asked, while acknowledging the group.
  • Virtual Interviews: Conducted via video conferencing. Ensure good lighting, a professional background, and minimal distractions.

Common Interview Questions

  • "Tell me about yourself."
    Provide a brief overview of your background, focusing on experiences most relevant to the role. Keep it to 1-2 minutes.
  • "Why are you interested in this position/company?"
    Demonstrate your research and genuine interest. Connect your skills and values to the organization's mission and the role.
  • "What are your strengths?"
    Choose strengths relevant to the role and provide specific examples that demonstrate them.
  • "What are your weaknesses?"
    Be honest but strategic. Choose a real area for growth and explain how you're working to improve it.
  • "Tell me about a time when..."
    Use the STAR method to structure your response with a clear situation, task, action, and result.
  • "Do you have any questions for us?"
    Always have thoughtful questions prepared. Ask about the role, team dynamics, company culture, or growth opportunities.

Interview Tips

  • Be Authentic: Be yourself. Authenticity is more valuable than trying to be what you think they want.
  • Listen Carefully: Pay attention to the question and answer what's being asked. It's okay to take a moment to think before responding.
  • Show Enthusiasm: Demonstrate genuine interest in the role and organization through your energy and engagement.
  • Use Examples: Support your answers with specific examples from your experiences, coursework, or projects.
  • Ask for Clarification: If you don't understand a question, it's better to ask for clarification than to guess.
  • Follow Up: Send a thank-you email within 24 hours of your interview, expressing appreciation and reiterating your interest.

Interview FAQ

  • How should I dress for an interview?
    Research the company culture, but when in doubt, err on the side of professional. Business casual is often appropriate, but some industries may require business professional attire.
  • What if I don't know the answer to a question?
    It's okay to admit you don't know something. Be honest, but also explain how you would approach finding the answer or learning the skill.
  • How do I handle nerves during an interview?
    Preparation is the best antidote to nerves. Practice your answers, research the company, and remember that the interviewer wants you to succeed. Take deep breaths and remember to listen.
  • Should I send a thank-you note?
    Yes! Send a personalized thank-you email within 24 hours to each person who interviewed you. Reference something specific from your conversation.
