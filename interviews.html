Interview Preparation

Prepare for Your Best Interview

Interviews can feel daunting, but with the right preparation you can approach them with confidence. This page covers different interview types, the STAR method for answering behavioral questions, and practical tips to help you showcase your skills and fit. Schedule a mock interview with a CDO Career Coach to practice and get feedback.

Behavioral Interviews

Employers use behavioral questions to understand how you have handled situations in the past. Use the STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result. Prepare 3–5 stories that highlight your skills and achievements.

Technical Interviews

For roles in UX, data, or development, you may face technical questions or exercises. Review core concepts, practice explaining your projects, and be ready to walk through your problem-solving process.

Virtual Interviews

Test your technology beforehand, ensure good lighting and a quiet space, and maintain eye contact with the camera. Dress professionally even for virtual settings.

Panel Interviews

You may meet with multiple interviewers at once. Address each person when answering, and try to learn each person's name and role at the start.

Common Interview Questions

Prepare concise, specific answers for questions like:

  • Tell me about yourself.
  • Why are you interested in this role/company?
  • Describe a time when you faced a challenge and how you overcame it.
  • What are your strengths and areas for growth?
  • Where do you see yourself in five years?
  • Do you have any questions for us?

Always prepare 2–3 thoughtful questions to ask the interviewer. This shows your interest and helps you evaluate if the role and organization are a good fit.
